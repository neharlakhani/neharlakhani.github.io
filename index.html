Image Driven Skin Cancer Diagnosis
Introduction and Background
Early detection is crucial for effective treatment of skin cancer, which is among the most
frequently diagnosed cancers worldwide. Historically, skin lesions are diagnosed by
dermatologists using visual examinations but new developments in ML methods show
potential for improving diagnostic precision.
Dataset
ISIC 2024 - Skin Cancer Detection with 3D-TBP
The collection includes isolated skin lesions taken from 3D full body images. Each lesion
image is cropped and labelled to indicate if the lesion is benign or malignant. The data also
includes signicant metadata, like the patient's age, gender, which can improve training of
the machine learning models. There are around 400,000 images in the dataset, which are
divided into training and testing sets.
Literature Survey
Skin cancer detection has signicantly beneted from the advancements in deep learning
methodologies, offering improved accuracy and eciency compared to traditional
diagnostic approaches.

Studies cited in [2] demonstrated that transfer learning signicantly enhances the

them on medical images, showcasing how transfer learning can compensate for the limited
availability of annotated medical data.
Problem Denition
Problem
Skin cancer is a widespread condition that requires early detection for an effective
treatment. Manual diagnosis performed by dermatologists relies on visual examination of
skin lesions. This examination can be extremely time-consuming and prone to human
errors, especially in areas with lack of medical expertise. The challenge is to develop an
automated system for malignancy prediction in skin lesions using their images.
Motivation
By building an ML model that can classify skin lesion images as cancerous or
non-cancerous, this project aims to:
Improve detection rates at early stages
Provide a second opinion for dermatologists to rely on
Provide access to regions lacking professional diagnostic services
